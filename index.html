Organisms

- plants, insects and mammals -

 

really suffer

 

when temperature

gets ramped up

a few degrees

above their normal niche.

 

But most species

- 90% or more -

are not perturbed really

by the current increase

in CO2 levels.

 

-------

 

Dr Ye Tao

Founder of MEER.org

 

In the next 50 years,

 

 assuming that we're emitting

and growing the economy

on current trajectory,

 

the predominant parameter is heat.

 

That's going to shut everything down.

 

But if we were somehow able to manage to prolong this fossil-fuel economy,

 

then by the 2070s 80s

 

ocean acidification

would also become a potentially dangerous environmental stressor

for marine ecosystems.

 

But it's highly unlikely for society

to really survive to that point

on our current emissions trajectory.

 

Therefore we really need to focus on decarbonizing safely

and also dealing with heat.
